Deep thoughts by Rhyan*:

-11:00 AM Update-

Overall, yeah it's a bit windy, but with warm temperatures, good visibility and partly sunny skies, it ain't no bother today! And as a bonus, there's some really great windblown powder on the North Side of the mountain! Hellroaring Basin is now open to the public!

-10:00 AM Update-

Chair 1 is open. Chair 8 and Hellroaring Basin will also have a delayed opening today.

-9:30 AM Update-

Chair 1 will have a slightly delayed opening this morning.

-8:30 AM Update-

High winds are the name of the game this morning! (The summit weather station shows sustained speeds of 15 mph with gusts at 41 mph as of 8:00 AM. The forecast for today calls for winds speeds at 30 to 38 mph, with gusts as high as 55 mph.) Conditions are changing rapidly out here with intermittent open visibility, blowing snow, and even some sunshine coming in and out across the mountain. As always, keep an eye on our webcams for the most current visibility and cloud cover conditions on the mountain (though today, that status may not last long!)

-6:00 AM Report-

Today's weather will likely be similar to yesterday's, with the forecast showing a chance of snow in the morning, partly sunny skies and high winds. The groomed runs were a blast yesterday with some windblown powder and carveable corduroy! By mid-day the snow softened up to spring-like conditions on the lower mountain. Off-trail we have variable conditions with mostly icy hardpack and some windblown stashes as well.

There are tons of fun events coming up this week, with the final Wednesday Night race league tonight, the last days of night skiing for the season this weekend, and another Biggie Banks race coming up on Saturday! To get all the details on these events check out our Events Calendar and come join in the fun!

Treewell Safety

Skiing and snowboarding off the groomed runs and in deep powder snow is one of the most exciting and appealing parts of our sport. If you decide to leave the groomed trails, you are voluntarily accepting the risk of falling into tree wells or deep snow and suffocating.

A deep snow or tree well accident occurs when a rider or skier falls into an area of deep unconsolidated snow and becomes immobilized. The more the person struggles, the more entrapped in the snow they become. Deaths resulting from these kinds of accidents are referred to as a NARSID or Non-Avalanche Related Snow Immersion Death. Fortunately, these types of accidents are preventable.

WHAT YOU CAN DO TO PREVENT TREE WELL & DEEP SNOW ACCIDENTS


1. Avoid Deep Snow & Tree Areas
2. Always Ski with a Partner and Keep Your Partner in Sight
3. Ski & Ride in Control and Defensively
4. Have a Plan to Survive

This abbreviated information is from http://www.deepsnowsafety.org. Please visit their website to learn more about tree well safety.
